Instructions

The link below will take you to a dashboard that will allow you to filter and select individual tests from the blocks. NOTE: the data in the dashboard at present is completely made up.

You can click on any of the bar graphs to select a particular sub-set of the test data. For example, to only see results from Block O1, click on that bar in the Blocks area on the upper right. When you select a particular performer, submission, or block, the Ground Truth vs Plausibility graph will change, showing only that data.

The tests in IntPhys are internally divided into different categories, depending on their level of dynamic complexity, the number of objects, and whether there is an occluder present. You can also filter on these; for example, you can compare the results when there are different numbers of objects present.

The Ground Truth vs Plausibility graph in the middle shows the ground truth (either 0 for implausible or 1 for plausible) versus the submitted answer (a float value between 0 and 1). A perfect score would be to have all the results at the bottom right (0,0) or upper left (1,1). The graph is also selectable, so it may be of interest to see why various tests are returning a low value when ground truth is 1.
